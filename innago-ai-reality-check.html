Three structural reasons
Why 65% of the work resists AI acceleration

These aren't adoption failures — they're context problems. AI tools are only as effective as the information they can access. In these domains, they simply can't access enough to operate autonomously.

⚙️
Legacy Microservice Sprawl
Our codebase spans dozens of microservices across separate domains and repositories. Support and bug work requires tracing issues across service boundaries — cross-service context that no AI tool can yet ingest end-to-end. Engineers must manually connect the dots between services, databases, and queues before AI can help.
🔒
Infrastructure Complexity
DevSecOps and infra work (Kubernetes, CI/CD, auth, security, compliance) operates across config layers, cloud APIs, and live runtime state. AI tools don't have access to cluster state, secrets management, or the full infrastructure graph needed to automate reliably. This work demands real-time system awareness that AI tooling can't provide yet.
🧩
No End-to-End Agent Context
AI agents need connected context — database state, queue state, service topology, ticket history, customer data. Our support and infrastructure layers don't yet have the MCP integrations or agent tooling to give AI the full picture it needs. This is precisely what we're building now (see the roadmap below).

Closing the gap
Bringing AI into the hard zones

We're not waiting for the tools to evolve on their own. We're actively building the integrations and agent infrastructure to extend AI into support, infrastructure, and legacy domains — the 65% of work that currently resists acceleration.
